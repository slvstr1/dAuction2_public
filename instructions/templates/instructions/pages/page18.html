Transactions Overview

At the top, to the right of the Production Cost, is the Transaction Overview. In this overview, each line represents a transaction, with the top line showing the most recent transaction. The transactions are numbered (#) and the higher the number, the more recent the transaction. Sales, with a red (or, in print, dark gray) background, always have a positive number of Units and purchases, with a blue (or, in print, light gray) background, a negative number. {% if auction.itype == auction.FULL_INSTR %}

Thus, the Trading account of the Producer in the example in Figure 9 (on the previous pages) would look as in Figure 10. You can see that the Producer, with the last transaction, earned a Total of 40 ECU by selling 10 Units for a Price per Unit of 4 ECU, and, with the transaction before that, spend a Total of 24 ECU by buying 8 Units for a Price per Unit of 3 ECU, and so on. {% endif %}


Profit Overview

Under the Transaction Overview is the Profit Overview. It shows the Total Cost, the Total Trading Revenues, and the PROFIT.{% if treatment.require_units_demanded_on_PR %} If a Producer received penalties, they will also be shown here.{% endif %}

The Total Cost is thus the total production cost of the Units Sold. The Total Trading Revenues is the total earnings (if positive) or total costs (if negative) from trading (selling and buying). The PROFIT is then equal to the Total Trading Revenues minus the Total Cost{% if treatment.require_units_demanded_on_PR %}and, if there are any, minus the penalties{% endif %}.
