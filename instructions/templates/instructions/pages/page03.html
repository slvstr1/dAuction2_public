2. Explanation of the {% if treatment.educational %}Business Simulation{% else %}Trading Experiment{% endif %}

2.1 Basic information

Figure 1: Rounds {% if treatment.educational %}in the Business Simulation{% else %}and stages in the Trading Experiment{% endif %}

You will be a member of a group. Including you, there will be {% wordify treatment.get_total_players %} participants in your group. Group membership is assigned randomly. You will not know who is in the group with you and none of your group members will know that you are in his or her group. In other words, group membership is anonymous. You will not interact with members of other groups. Your group membership stays fixed for the experiment.

In the {% if treatment.educational %}Business Simulation{% else %}Trading Experiment{% endif %}, each member of the group will make decisions in a market. There will be {{ treatment.total_periods }} rounds in total. {% if treatment.pay_one_random_period %} {% if treatment.educational %} One of the rounds will be selected at random and the earnings of this round will be paid to you at the end of the Business Game. {% else %} One of the rounds will be selected at random and the earnings of this round will be paid to you at the end of the experiment. {% endif %} {% else %} Your earnings over all rounds will be added up and the total will be paid to you in the end of the experiment. {% endif %}

In each round there will be {% if treatment.only_spot %}a spot market.{% else %} two market stages, first {{ forward_market }} and then {{ spot_market }}.{% endif %} The time in a {% if treatment.educational %}market{% else %}stage{% endif %} can be extended by a CONDITIONAL TIME PERIOD and{% if not treatment.only_spot %}, in {{ spot_market }}, {% endif %} by a PENALTY TIME PERIOD. We explain the extending time periods in detail below.
