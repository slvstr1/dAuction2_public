The MarginalCostOfAggregateMissingUnits is calculated as follows. At the end of the round, the aggregate total of missing units over all Retailers in the group is calculated. We then calculate the cost of producing the last unit of the aggregate total of missing units if they were produced by the Producers in your group.

In other words, we divide the aggregate total of missing units over the Producers such that the total production of Producers is as equal as possible, keeping in mind the amount Producers already produced in the round. We then look at the cost of producing the last unit of the aggregate total of missing units. This cost we call the MarginalCostOfAggregateMissingUnits.



The financial penalty for Retailers {% if treatment.require_units_demanded_on_PR %}and Producers{% endif %} are thus very large but can be avoided by buying {% if treatment.require_units_demanded_on_PR %}(in the role of Retailer) or selling (in the role of Producer){% endif %} a number of units equal to the number of UNITS DEMANDED.
